Welcome to the Meteorite Landings API

Introduction

This project visualizes meteorite landings around the world using data obtained from NASA's Meteorite Landings dataset. The data is used to generate multiple visualizations, including line charts tracking meteorite sightings over the years. The visualizations are served through a Flask-based API.

Longitude and Latitude by Year

In the historical context, meteorite sightings were sparse, but they became increasingly concentrated in specific regions over time, particularly in the 19th and 20th centuries, which is evident in the longitude and latitude maps above. This trend has likely been influenced by both advancements in technology for observing meteorites and changes in reporting practices.

Meteorite Sightings Trend (1900-2021)

Over the course of the 20th and 21st centuries, meteorite sightings and landings have become more frequent. The heatmap below illustrates the distribution of meteorite landings by year group and meteorite class. Some key points include:

  • There was a significant increase in meteorite observations and landings after the 1960s due to advancements in satellite and telescope technology.
  • The meteorite classes that dominate the dataset are typically stony meteorites, such as the L6 and H5 types, which are composed of silicate minerals.
  • In the more recent decades (2000-2021), meteorite sightings have spiked, likely due to the increase in citizen science efforts, media coverage of meteor showers, and more widespread use of technology to detect meteorite events.
